Common Questions

Before you book.

The questions we're asked most often by new guests. If your question isn't here, send us a note — we answer every message personally.

How far in advance should I book?
For weekend appointments, we recommend booking two to three weeks ahead. Weekday mornings and afternoons usually have more flexibility, often within the same week. For new color clients and major changes, we block longer windows, so those slots fill earliest.
Do you offer free consultations?
Yes — every new guest receives a complimentary twenty-minute consultation. Sit with one of our artists, share your hair history and what you're hoping for, and leave with a clear plan. There's no pressure to book at the end of the consultation.
What should I bring to my first appointment?
Come with your hair in the state you wear it most — no fresh wash, no heavy product. Bring two or three inspiration photos if you have them. Your stylist will translate the shapes and tones to work with your hair density and face shape.
What is your cancellation policy?
We ask for forty-eight hours' notice on any cancellation or reschedule. Late cancellations and no-shows are charged fifty percent of the booked service, which helps us respect the time our stylists have set aside for you.
Do you accept walk-ins?
We don't. Every chair at Velo is booked by appointment, so each guest gets the stylist's full attention for the full duration of their service. Fringe trims between appointments with your regular stylist are the one exception — those are complimentary and you can walk in between 10 and 5.
Can you work with all hair types and textures?
Yes. Our artists specialize across the full texture spectrum — from fine straight hair to coily and natural textures. When you reach out, let us know what kind of hair you have and we'll match you with the stylist whose specialty fits. Our texture program is led by Kassa, one of our senior artists.
